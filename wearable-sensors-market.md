<p><p>The wearable sensors market is divided into two main types: invasive and non-invasive sensors. Invasive sensors are embedded within the body to monitor health metrics, providing precise data, often used in clinical settings. Conversely, non-invasive wearable sensors are worn externally, such as fitness trackers and smartwatches, to track various health parameters like heart rate, sleep patterns, and physical activity without the need for surgical intervention. Both types serve vital roles in health monitoring and management, catering to different user needs.</p></p>
