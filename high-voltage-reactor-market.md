<p><ul><li>Series Reactor</li><li>Shunt Reactor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The High Voltage Reactor market is primarily divided into two types: series reactors and shunt reactors. Series reactors are used to limit short-circuit currents and improve system stability by adding inductance in series with the circuit. Shunt reactors, on the other hand, are employed to compensate for reactive power and regulate voltage levels, particularly in long transmission lines. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing the reliability and efficiency of electrical power systems in high-voltage applications.</p></p>
