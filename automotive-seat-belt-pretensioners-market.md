<p><p>Automotive seat belt pretensioners are safety devices designed to tighten seat belts during a collision, minimizing occupant movement. The market primarily consists of two types: mechanical and electrical. Mechanical pretensioners utilize a spring or inertia-based mechanism that activates through the vehicle's crash dynamics, while electrical pretensioners employ electronic signals to trigger the system, often integrated with advanced airbag systems. Both types enhance safety but differ in their activation methods and complexity, influencing automotive design and deployment strategies.</p></p>
