<p><p>The automotive brake caliper market is primarily divided into two types: floating and fixed brake calipers. Floating brake calipers consist of one or two pistons located on one side, allowing the caliper to slide and clamp the brake pads against the disc, providing effective braking with simpler construction. Fixed brake calipers, on the other hand, have pistons on both sides, ensuring more consistent force distribution and enhanced braking performance. Both types are essential for vehicle safety and efficiency, catering to various automotive applications.</p></p>
