<p><p>The Nitinol spring market comprises three primary types: compression springs, extension springs, and torsion springs. Compression springs absorb axial loads and are designed to compress under force, commonly used in automotive and medical devices. Extension springs generate tension when stretched, often utilized in applications requiring force balance, such as in machinery and electronics. Torsion springs store energy and exert rotational force, typically found in appliances and various mechanical devices. Each type leverages Nitinol's unique shape memory and superelastic properties for enhanced performance.</p></p>
